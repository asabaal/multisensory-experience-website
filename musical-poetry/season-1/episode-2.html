Episode 2 — CHILD OF GOD (WHO YOU BE)

Musical Poetry with Asabaal is an audio blog series exploring the intersections of faith, identity, theology, and lived experience through music and spoken word. Each entry pairs a featured piece with reflection and commentary.

2026-02-13

Opening Reflection

The Scripture is unclear on a number of things, but on this, it is completely clear. As it says in the letter to the Romans,

"All who are led by the Spirit of God are children of God",

and in the letter to the Galatians:

"Now that faith has come, we are no longer subject to a disciplinarian, for in Christ Jesus you are all children of God through faith…there is no longer male and female",

But it's that last phrase that people typically fight over.

Featured Lyrics Excerpt

I don't know who you be, I'm a child of God
Just cuz you also free, don't mean I am not

Title: CHILD OF GOD (WHO YOU BE)

Project: I Never Asked to Be Queer

Format: Musical Poetry / Spoken Word

Commentary

I didn't waste time getting right to the point here. If we are to take the phrase "there is no longer male and female" as literal truth, then it is completely nonsensical to fight against queer rights. Why are you fighting against the removal and redefinition of labels that our own sacred texts tell us don't carry any weight anymore?

Getting to the point where I could write this song took a lot of work. I grew up in what I now know to be a region heavily influenced by white supremacist Christian Nationalism, and it is precisely these people who taught me to fear queer folk while simultaneously providing the theological framework for acceptance, so that when I was physically separated from that environment, I was able to discern the truth - that all humans are created in the image of God, and that since the quality of queerness has both biological and environmental factors that contribute to its emergence, that God never wanted us to fight against something that doesn't take us away from God.

And being queer, and in my particular case, also trans, is not something that takes one away from God. I have witnessed many a rainbow child of God walk themselves out of faith because of the hatred of many in these types of traditions, hatred that was endured or witnessed. Then these stories are weaponized against the entirety of the demographic rather than responsibility being taken by those that caused the harm leading to lack of faith.

I almost fell in the same trap, the one laid out by the enemy of our souls. For a long time, I struggled to feel God's presence near me because I was angry at God for allowing the people that claimed to be God's people to cause such harm to myself and others, even before I acknowledged my own queerness.

I won't judge people. That is God's role. I moved on from defending my identity and went to declaring it as simply, part of me. And my identifying characteristics are what uniquely qualify me to serve God's purpose for me rather than something or someone else filling the role. So yes, this piece belongs as part of my story, but it is still in the identity formation phase, which is why it belongs on I Never Asked To Be Queer.

I never asked to be queer. I did ask for space to explore, to understand the way I function. And after I had an idea of what that was, I asked for purpose that I could walk in and be exactly who God has for me to be.

Take a listen to this song. Dwell on the lyrics. Spend some time in prayer. If you aren't someone who acknowledges the inherent value that queer people bring to reality and to the body of Christ, I ask you, why? A theological difference? Do you even know me? God is my everything. Life without God, for me, is no life at all. I tried it. It failed me. I really do believe. Take your time to grieve. I'm not trying to please you.

Closing Reflection

Child of God. That's exactly who I am. Who you be?
